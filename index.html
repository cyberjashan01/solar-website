How Solar Panels Work

  • Solar cells convert sunlight into electricity
  • DC current is converted to AC using an inverter
  • Energy is stored or used for appliances
  • Excess energy can be fed to the grid

Maintenance of Solar Panels

Regular maintenance of solar panels is essential to ensure optimal performance and longevity. Here are some key practices to follow:

  • 1. Regular Cleaning:

    Clean the panels every 1-3 months to remove dust, dirt, and debris. This ensures maximum sunlight absorption and efficiency.

  • 2. Monitor for Shade:

    Regularly check for any new obstructions, such as trees or buildings, that may cast shadows on the panels, reducing their efficiency.

  • 3. Inspect for Damage:

    Conduct visual inspections for cracks, loose connections, or other damage. Address any issues promptly to prevent further complications.

  • 4. Performance Monitoring:

    Utilize monitoring tools to track energy output. If you notice a significant drop in performance, investigate potential causes.

  • 5. Schedule Professional Inspections:

    Engage a professional to conduct a thorough inspection at least once a year. This ensures all components are functioning optimally.
